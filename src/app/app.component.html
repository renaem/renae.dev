WEARABLE SAFETY TECH

Lead Product Designer

As Lead Product Designer for MakuSafe, I design software for a unique problem space: hazard detection in industrial workplaces.

MakuSafe provides an innovative hardware ecosystem consisting of base station kiosks, wearable devices, and of course—MakuSmart, the supporting cloud data & analytics platform.

Taking Certified Occupational Safety Specialist (COSS) training early on allowed me to gain new personal understanding of the concerns of a safety professional. This training not only fundamentally influenced my design perspective, but also supported me to speak knowledgeably while consulting our primary users: industrial safety professionals.

My work has included several large projects such as a major software 2.0 redesign, design system implementation, new feature development, feature redesigns, mobile app design, reporting, data visualization, brand materials, and more.

I actively participate as a full stack designer / product engineer: defining technical requirements and leading front-end development on our software team; seeing every project through from conception to iteration to completion and beyond.

Frontline Worker iOS App

Coming soon. My latest project is the native mobile application that allows workers to pair with their wearable device, receive MakuSmart notifications, and view their data contributions.

The Motion Explorer was designed to assist safety leadership in uncovering potential hazardous motion issues within their workforce, and remains a flagship feature.

Machine learning models analyze motion across each worker's day, classifying individuals who may be susceptible to sustained motion injuries and providing the contextual data for each classification.

This feature was an instant success upon launch in Fall 2021, providing daily classifications and actionable information to busy safety leaders. I'm incredibly proud to be able to say that our work here contributed to a reduction of injuries by 50% to 90%.

ROLL FOR INITIATIVE

Kickstarter & Product Design

A successful Kickstarter project involving a redesign of a Dungeons and Dragons (DnD) platform called GMBinder. The site features “homebrew” content: material written by users from all over the world, intended to be used as the setting and story for tabletop role playing games—the most popular of which being DnD. These documents are authored using Markdown and processed on-the-fly into beautifully formatted documents. I not only revamped the UI, but created custom illustrations and textures for a new document theme and other promotional Kickstarter materials.
